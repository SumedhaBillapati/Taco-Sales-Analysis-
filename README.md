# 🌮 Taco Sales Analysis
### An Entrepreneur's Path to the Ideal Taco Market in the US

*Presented at AI Fantastic, UMass Boston · Used as teaching material at Fisher College*

---

## The Setup

Imagine you want to open a taco business in the US. You have ambition, a budget, and a love for tacos — but no idea where to start.

Which city do you pick? What tacos do people actually want? When do they order? What should you charge?

This project answers all of that. I placed myself in the shoes of a first-time food entrepreneur and used real sales data to build a market entry strategy from scratch.

---

## The Questions I Set Out to Answer

- Which US city has the highest taco demand?
- What taco types and sizes sell the most?
- When do people eat tacos, and does it change by day?
- How does pricing vary across markets?
- Can toppings be a revenue strategy?

---

## What the Data Said

**City:** Chicago came out on top for order volume, and sits in a medium price range, making it the ideal sweet spot between high demand and lower competition compared to LA's saturated premium market.

**Taco Type:** Chicken and Veggie tacos are the top sellers in Chicago. Beef sits at the higher price end, Veggie at the lower.

**Size:** Large tacos account for 56% of orders, Chicago is a "big taco" market.

**Timing:** Saturday is the busiest day. Chicken tacos dominate mid-week; Veggie tacos spike on weekends in large sizes, useful for stocking decisions.

**Toppings:** More toppings = higher price, with a clear positive correlation. Business insight: keep base tacos affordable, and profit from add-ons.

---

## The Recommendation

Open in **Chicago.** Price in the **$7-9 medium range.** Lead with **Chicken and Veggie tacos in large sizes.** Build your margin through **toppings and customization.**

## Beyond the Project: This Became a Teaching Tool

What started as a personal practice project ended up in two guest lecture settings at Fisher College:

**Guest Lecture 1: Critical Thinking in the Age of AI**
I used this project to walk students through what it actually looks like to think critically with data. Not just what tools to use, but what questions to ask, how to challenge your assumptions, and how bringing a business owner's mindset changes the analysis you do and the conclusions you reach. The taco entrepreneur framing made abstract thinking tangible.

**Workshop: Data Visualization with Tableau**
In a follow-up session, I introduced students to Tableau using this dataset as the foundation. Students worked hands-on with the data, building their own visualizations and learning how to turn numbers into decisions. The goal was for them to leave the room having done something, not just watched something.

Both sessions reinforced something I believe: good analysis is only useful if you can communicate it to someone who wasn't in the room when you built it.
